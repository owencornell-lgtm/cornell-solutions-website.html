The problem

Most outbound burns the thing it depends on.

Generic blasts torch domain reputation before they ever book a meeting. The list goes stale, the rep guesses, and the inbox quietly stops listening.

Typical outbound
  • One shared domain — every send puts the others at risk
  • The same message to every lead on the list
  • Volume pushed until the domain gets flagged
  • A hot reply sits unanswered for a day or more
Cornell, engineered
  • Isolated sending domains for every single client
  • Every message written from real lead research
  • Fixed caps — scaled by adding inboxes, not cranking them
  • Warm voice and SMS follow up the moment someone replies
The system

Four channels. One sequence.

Every lead is researched first — then moves through four channels, in order, until it replies.

STEP 00 · THE FOUNDATION

Research

Every lead is profiled before a single message goes out — company, role, recent signals, and the reason it's worth reaching out at all. The four channels only work because the research comes first.

CHANNEL 01

Email

Personalized sends from warmed, isolated domains — capped to protect deliverability, not chase volume.

CHANNEL 02

LinkedIn

A parallel touch that reinforces the email and gives the lead a second reason to reply.

CHANNEL 03

Warm voice

The instant a lead replies interested, an AI voice agent calls, qualifies, and books the meeting.

CHANNEL 04

SMS

A short, well-timed text on the warmest replies — the nudge that turns "interested" into a booked call.

The doctrine

Deliverability is the business.

A booked meeting means nothing if it cost you the inbox that booked it. Three rules we don't bend on.

  • 01

    Domain isolation

    Every client sends from their own domains — never shared, never mixed with another reputation.

  • 02

    Sending discipline

    A fixed daily cap per inbox. More reach comes from more inboxes, never a higher number.

  • 03

    Continuous monitoring

    Inbox health is tracked live. Sending pauses automatically, well before reputation is at risk.
